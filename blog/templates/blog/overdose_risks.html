What Is an Overdose?

An overdose occurs when a person takes more than the medically recommended dose of a drug, which can lead to harmful effects.

Common Signs of Overdose

Signs may include difficulty breathing, loss of consciousness, and extreme confusion. Recognizing these symptoms can save lives!

Combining Drugs Increases Risk

Mixing drugs, whether prescription or recreational, can significantly increase the risk of overdose. Always consult a healthcare professional.

What to Do in Case of an Overdose

Call emergency services immediately if you suspect an overdose. Prompt action can make a difference in outcomes.
