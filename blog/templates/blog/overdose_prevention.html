Preventing Overdose: What You Need to Know

Understanding Overdose

An overdose occurs when a person takes more than the recommended amount of a substance, leading to dangerous health effects.

Signs of Overdose

Common signs of overdose include:
  • Unresponsiveness or inability to wake up
  • Slow or irregular breathing
  • Blue or purple skin, especially on lips and nails
  • Choking or gurgling sounds
  • Severe confusion or disorientation

How to Prevent Overdose

Tips to prevent overdose:
  • Always follow the prescribed dosage from a healthcare provider.
  • Avoid mixing substances, especially alcohol with other drugs.
  • Keep track of how much you or others are taking.
  • Educate yourself and others about the risks of substance use.
  • Be aware of your own tolerance and the potency of the substance.

What to Do if Someone is Overdosing

Steps to take:
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Try to keep the person awake and sitting up.
  • If they are unresponsive, place them in the recovery position.
  • Stay with the person until help arrives.
  • If you have naloxone (Narcan), administer it if trained to do so.
